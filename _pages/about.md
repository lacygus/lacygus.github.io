I am a master's student in **Applied Statistics & Data Science at UCLA** (GPA 4.0/4.0),
with a B.S. in Data Science from UC San Diego. My work sits at the intersection of
**large language models, natural language processing, and applied statistics** — I build
end-to-end pipelines that turn messy, real-world interaction data into rigorous,
reproducible inference.

My current research uses **LLM multi-agent simulations** to study how the structural
design of team discussions shapes both how teams collaborate and the quality of what they
produce. The thesis pairs ~24,000 utterances from real research teams against matched
agent simulations under controlled conditions, evaluated with a 55-metric NLP framework
and a multi-judge LLM ensemble. A paper from this work is **under review at EMNLP 2026**.

In summer 2025 I was an **AI Model Research Intern at Samsung Electronics**, where I
researched multimodal LLM merging — designing layer-wise strategies and a scalable
safetensors merging pipeline that raised language-benchmark scores while preserving
multimodal performance.

Research interests
======
- LLM multi-agent systems and agent-based simulation of human collaboration
- NLP for team communication: interaction metrics, conversation networks, topic dynamics
- Multimodal model merging and evaluation
- Reproducible statistical inference for small-N, real-world experimental designs
